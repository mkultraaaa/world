A note on how the deck was made

Not a brochure.
A working document.

Venara — twenty-seven residences on the Paphos coast, 500 m from Venus Beach. This page describes how the seventeen-slide sales deck for Venara was put together: what is in it, on what basis, and what was deliberately left out.

01What it is

A seventeen-slide deck in English.

EN-only. Editorial palette — cream, ink, copper. Cormorant Garamond and JetBrains Mono. Designed for one purpose: to be sent to a serious buyer (or their lawyer) and to hold attention from cover to contact without a single banned word.

The seventeen slides are deliberately tight. An earlier draft ran to twenty-five; the final cut removed slides that repeated themselves and merged location into the invitation. What stayed is what does work.

02The spine

Three verbs, one investor.

Own it. Rent it. Live in it.
The axis · on the cover · investor first

The developer’s product is an apart-hotel — twenty-seven residences run by an in-house management company. The buyer profile (clarified directly by the developer) is a pure investor seeking passive income; nationality and age vary, the goal does not.

The deck reflects that. The axis leads with own, then rent, then live. Lifestyle is real, but it is not first. Every slide answers in this order: where, what, how it works, who runs it, what it costs, what is around it, when it opens.

03What it was based on

Real inputs, in order of weight.

  • developerDirect interviews with Rinat Khissamov (Motive Point) — voice messages over two weeks, transcribed and treated as canon for facts.
  • proposalThe internal investment proposal for unit B101 — twenty-four pages with full financial model, real expense breakdown, and methodology. Source for every price and IRR figure considered.
  • competitorsSeven Cyprus development decks analysed slide-by-slide: Cybarco Thalassa and Trilogy, Pafilia, INEX (MOREA), Imperio, BBF, Korantina. Decisions trace to specific patterns each does well.
  • copyIlyakhov’s rules adapted to English luxury copy: numbers over adjectives, verbs over nominalisations, one idea per sentence, proof next to claim. A list of thirty banned words was used as a filter.
  • photographyExisting TAD-studio renders — thirty-four assets. Reviewed for fit; the strongest carry the deck. Two further renders (twilight and lifestyle) are recommended but not commissioned.

05Numbers behind the numbers

What the yield figure is built on.

The deck states a target net IRR range of seven to nine percent across the portfolio. That range is not aspiration. It is anchored to a single, real, currently-operating apartment.

Base

2025 rental actuals of a two-bedroom apartment in Elysia Park, Paphos, owned by a shareholder of Motive Point. Booking.com and Airbnb monthly statements verifiable on request.

Location coefficient

+83% — derived from nightly-rate comparison between Downtown Park (location analogue) and Elysia Park.

Size coefficient

−33% — derived from 1BR-vs-2BR nightly rates within Elysia Park, managed by Orkadial Developments.

For the sample one-bedroom (B101, 50 m², €275,000 + 19% VAT refundable, €300,000 fully furnished), the model returns gross revenue €42,545, net profit €26,519, and a net IRR of 8.84%. Per-unit projections are delivered alongside each shortlist — not in this deck.

06What stayed out

What this deck does not do.

No invented stats

An earlier draft contained "Founded 2018 / 120+ residences delivered" — none of which was verified. Removed. The developer slide now describes only what is true.

No anonymous quotes

An earlier draft used a fabricated architect quote ("we wanted a building that would age slowly"). Removed. Attribution-or-nothing.

No banned vocabulary

Thirty words filtered out: luxury, exclusive, prestigious, world-class, stunning, breathtaking, jewel, haven, masterpiece, refined, and the rest of the usual list. Every adjective was forced to defend itself.

No per-unit numbers

Pricing and yield for individual units are deliberately held back for the personalised follow-up. The general deck names the range; the model is delivered when there is a real buyer to model for.
07What the research surfaced

Decisions traced to evidence.

  • axis flipInvestor-first ordering ("Own. Rent. Live.") emerged from the developer's own framing of the buyer — pure investor, lifestyle secondary. The earlier "Live. Rent. Own." was poetic but mis-aimed.
  • cyprus frameworkAdded after pre-mortem identified the deck would lose non-EU buyers without a single page on residency, tax, and stamp duty.
  • ice bathOne word, surfaced from the developer's own proposal — a hot/cold protocol signal absent from competitor decks on the same coast.
  • no hotels claimVerified, then made specific: five existing hotels within 1 km of Venus Beach, all over twenty years old, none with year-round heated pool and ice bath.
  • vat clarityStating "VAT refundable under License to Use scheme" up front filters out the +19% surprise that derails Cyprus deals at deposit stage.
